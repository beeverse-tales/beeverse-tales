The Zebra at the Wedding

Everyone was already there, or let’s say, almost everyone. Dressed in elegant black and white, they clinked glasses and smiled for the cameras. The wedding had started. Smooth. Sophisticated. Perfect...

Until it wasn’t. There was a moment of silence, one of those in-between breaths when she walked in. A young lady in a zebra-print outfit. She walked in like a myth. Striped in defiance, smiling in ruins. When it came to dressing, she understood the dress code. She didn’t come here to play. She was tall, dark, dripping supermodel energy. Her heels echoed like drumbeats on marble. She didn’t whisper. She didn’t greet. She sat in the back, alone, hat low over her face. Still, the wedding was never the same again. The bride leaned toward her bridesmaid. “Who invited the zebra?” “I don’t know… probably one of those people who show up uninvited.” In a way, she was right. No one invited her. But everyone saw her. The ceremony went on — barely. Eyes darted, whispers swirled. But the zebra never moved. Never spoke. Then came the end. The guests began to leave. And the paparazzi swarmed… but not around the bride. Not the groom. All cameras turned to the girl in stripes. They asked who she was, and she told them. Told them everything. How the bride, once her best friend, had stolen her boyfriend. How she found out about the wedding from a gossip page. How she walked in with silence and stripes, not for revenge, but for closure. By the time the bride and groom realized what was happening, half the guests were already gone. Some didn’t even say goodbye. The headlines that night were loud and clear: “THE ZEBRA GIRL AT THE WEDDING.” Her face uncovered. Her truth unveiled. And the bride? Ashamed. Labeled a boyfriend snatcher. The wedding, now trending as the worst ever. Days passed. The shame wouldn’t fade. They couldn’t silence the tweets, the TikToks, the threads. Eventually, they had no choice but to come clean. They invited the zebra girl. And apologized, in public. But the internet never forgets. And neither did she.

    I Didn't Know She Was My Biological Mum

    ... inside her house was a family portrait, she looked so happy on the picture but looked so sad in real life. There was also an old calendar of 2002, in the calendar, 8th March was marked with a red pen. She came with two glasses of wine, one for me and the other one for her. We shared some talks, and she seemed like she was forcing herself to laugh.

    She noticed the curiosity in my face and told me, "I can see there's something on your mind, you're free to ask." "Why do you look so sad?" I asked. She glared at the portrait and then at the calendar, her eyes watery. "You see... That portrait, that's my husband, my daughter, and I. That date marked on the calendar was supposed to be my best day ever, my first birthday to celebrate with my husband and daughter. We were driving towards town to an unknown place, listening to soft music, when suddenly… an accident happened. My daughter died on the spot, and my husband didn’t make it either. From that day, I became a shipwrecked woman with no one to care for. So you see… that day, I'll never forget it."

    I felt sorry for her. At 25, she was already a widow and had lost a child. I offered her a shoulder to cry on and kept telling her it would be okay. To console her differently, I began reading a book aloud. She started laughing — thank goodness, this is what I wanted.

    "Didn't you consider remarrying? I mean, it's 22 years since that happened, you deserve happiness," I asked. She glared at me, tears in her eyes, "My daughter… I loved my family so much, I couldn't replace it. But the real reason… after the accident, my womb was damaged. The doctor told me I would never bear children again. That was the saddest thing in my life, so I didn’t see a reason to remarry."

    She continued, "Everything happens for a reason, but sometimes I ask, why me? I wish my other daughter hadn’t died at birth." I interrupted, "Wait… you had another daughter?" "Yes," she said, "I gave birth to twins, but one didn’t make it. You remind me so much of them."

    I asked, "When were they born?" "2nd of February 2002," she replied. My mind raced. Why would we share a birthday? Could it be… a hospital mix-up? I suggested a DNA test to clear my doubts. She agreed.

    We went to the hospital and waited a week for results. On the day, she saw the results, covered her mouth, and tears of joy fell. She hugged me tightly and shouted, "Welcome back, my daughter! I can't believe I've always thought you were dead, thanks for coming back to my rescue." I cried too. "Thank you for being my mother. I won't leave you, mum, till infinity… AND BEYOND."

      Chronicles of Queen Bee and Mimi 1

      Hey there! My name is Bee — but around here, they call me Queen Bee. I’m 23, a proud cat lover, and a software developer by trade. My life? A whole lot of tech, and a whole lot of feline drama. And of course, I don’t live alone — I share my space with Mimi, my sassy, snuggly, four-pawed princess...

      Meet Queen Bee and Mimi

      Mimi isn’t just a cat — she’s my little princess. Our relationship is equal parts chaos and cuddles. She keeps me company, makes me laugh, and brightens even the dullest of debugging days. Working remotely as a developer means I’m home most of the time, and honestly, that’s been the best thing for both of us.

      She’s a snob when she wants to be, a bully when she’s bored, but always full of affection when I need it most. She knows when I’m sad and leans against my shoulder, her soft fur comforting me more than any words could. And yes — she has her own cat bed, but somehow always ends up in mine.

      Life as a Remote Worker and Cat Mama

      People think working from home is easy. A friend once told me it’s “just chilling and waiting for payment.” But being a software developer is no joke. Coding is fun, but it’s demanding. And being a cat mum? That requires patience, empathy, and learning to read a language with no words.

      My mornings usually start with coffee at 9 a.m., while Mimi enjoys her second breakfast of the day — a soft life, right? She’s disciplined too, always sensing when it’s mealtime. Once the princess is served, I get back to debugging until noon. After that, the afternoons are ours: playtime, cuddles, and mood swings galore.

      This rhythm — code, coffee, cuddles — makes me grateful every day. Being a software engineer and a cat mum at the same time feels like living two dream lives at once.

        House of Secrets

        Everyone hides under a mask. My name is Julian — Julie to most. I live with my big sister, Elle, a stylist with more secrets than she lets on. My best friend Benita? The one I tell everything. And Rommy — my ex who once swore I’d never find someone like him. He was right. I don’t want anyone like him. But today feels different. Bright. Hopeful. Until secrets begin to unravel...

        Behind the Masks

        My mornings are stitched together by Elle’s routine — her coffee, her warnings, her protectiveness. She drives me to lectures on her way to work. Today, Benita was waiting at the computer hub, bubbling about a party at Apartment K. My attention drifted to a guy passing by — Miles, handsome, confident, and nothing like Rommy. One wink, one smile, and I was distracted.

        We sat together in class. He asked for my number, asked if I’d be at the party. I said yes, though convincing Elle wouldn’t be easy. Benita teased me for already falling again, but I brushed it off. This felt different.

        The Party and the Crash

        Elle let me go on one condition — she’d drop me off and pick me up by ten. At the party, Miles was there. We talked, laughed, danced. I sipped wine, he made me smile. When it ended, he left with friends in his car; I went home, carefree. Until morning news struck — an accident. His car. My heart froze.

        Miles survived, with a broken hand. But when I saw Benita whispering with Rommy, I overheard enough to know: the crash wasn’t an accident. Rommy had planned it. Benita helped. Betrayal cut sharper than any heartbreak.

        Truths Revealed

        I confronted her. She threatened me with my own secrets — the things Elle must never know. I ended our friendship right there. Later, at Miles’ house, he told me he loved me. I told him to heal first, though my heart was already answering.

        Back home, Elle was waiting. She smiled, a secret of her own ready to spill. “I have a confession,” she said. My chest tightened. “I am the Masked Girl.” My favorite singer, the one the world had never seen without a mask — was my sister. She was finally ready to reveal her face.

        And I realized then: everyone is hiding under a mask. The question is — what happens when they fall?
